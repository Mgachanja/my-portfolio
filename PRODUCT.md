# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: technical recruiters, hiring managers, and engineering leads scanning candidate portfolios, often on mobile, often giving a page under a minute before deciding whether to reach out. They arrive from a CV, LinkedIn, or a job application link, already knowing the name — the site's job is to confirm the claim and make contact effortless. Secondary: other developers (potential collaborators, freelance clients) evaluating actual build quality, who will look closer at code and technical detail.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Mark Gachanja Ngari, a full stack developer (React, Next.js, React Native, Node.js, Spring Boot, Laravel) based in Nairobi, Kenya, currently employed and open to full-time roles, contract work, and freelance projects. It exists to convert a passive CV read into a direct conversation. Success is a recruiter or client clicking through to WhatsApp, email, or a phone call within the first visit — not admiring the design in isolation.

## Positioning

A production developer who has led real migrations (React 16 → 18) on live systems, not a bootcamp portfolio of tutorial clones — proven by specific, verifiable work rather than inflated metrics.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: message directly — tap-to-WhatsApp, tap-to-call, and tap-to-email, all one tap away, no contact form.
- Secondary CTA: download CV (PDF) and view GitHub.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: this person ships production React work and is reachable in one tap.
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a real working developer, not a template portfolio — the code-editor hero and specific technical detail prove it; (2) the experience is real and current, not padded — one employer, one migration story, told plainly; (3) reaching out is effortless — contact is one tap away with no form to fill.
- Proof on hand: `src/assets/Mark Gachanja Ngari CV.pdf` (source of truth for all resume facts); real employment at Cenco Consultoria (Mar 2024–present, React 16→18 migrations); Arpella Stores freelance project (web + iOS + Android e-commerce platform); GitHub at github.com/Mgachanja.

## Brand Personality

Precise, confident, modern. The voice of someone who writes clean commits and doesn't over-explain: short, declarative, specific numbers and technologies over vague adjectives. Confidence comes from specificity ("led React 16→18 migrations"), never from hype language ("passionate ninja rockstar"). No apology, no filler, no emoji.

## Anti-references

No sci-fi/Star Wars theming or crawl intros — the previous version's defining flaw. No generic AI-SaaS template look: no purple-to-blue gradient hero, no glassmorphism cards stacked everywhere, no stock-photo-style placeholder screenshots presented as real product shots (the previous version reused byte-identical images across two different "projects"). No fabricated or unverifiable metrics (the previous copy claimed specific percentage improvements with no source — everything shown must trace back to the CV or be honestly framed as exploratory). No skill progress bars with invented percentages. No contact form as the primary contact path — direct tap-to-contact only.

## Design Principles

- Show, don't claim: every credibility signal (the migration story, the multi-platform project, the tech stack) is specific and checkable, never a generic superlative.
- One tap to a human: email, phone, and WhatsApp are real `mailto:` / `tel:` / `wa.me` links everywhere they appear, not decorative icons.
- The craft is the pitch: since the product being sold is frontend skill itself, the build quality of this very page (animation, responsiveness, performance) is part of the evidence, not separate from it.
- Honest about scale: this is an early-career developer with one real employer and one flagship project, presented with full confidence at that actual scale — no padding with invented projects or teams.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: 4.5:1 body text contrast, visible keyboard focus states on every interactive element, semantic landmarks and heading order, alt text on all meaningful images. `prefers-reduced-motion` must be respected with a crossfade/instant fallback for every animation (scroll reveals, hero typing effect, marquee).
